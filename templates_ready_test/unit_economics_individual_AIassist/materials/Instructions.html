1. Important Disclaimer

This is a simulation task, not a real business operation, investment decision, hiring decision, customer interaction, legal process, medical process, or financial advisory activity.

All business scenarios, characters, organizations, data, AI outputs, and decision problems are created for educational and research purposes only. Your decisions in this session will not affect any real company, customer, employee, investor, or participant.

Please do not enter private, sensitive, confidential, proprietary, medical, financial, or personally identifying information into the simulation unless the research team explicitly instructs you to do so.

2. Why You Are Being Asked to Participate

You are being invited to participate in a simulation-based study about how people make decisions, evaluate information, and work with or without AI assistance in structured business or organizational tasks.

The purpose of the session is to understand how participants reason through tasks, how they respond to AI-generated suggestions, and how decision quality may change across different stages of the simulation.

Your participation will help researchers improve simulation design, AI-assisted learning tools, decision-support systems, and team training methods.

3. Voluntary Participation

Your participation is voluntary.

You may choose not to participate. You may stop participating at any time. You may skip any question or task that makes you uncomfortable, unless the facilitator explains that the task is required for session completion.

Choosing not to participate or stopping early will not result in penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. If this simulation is part of a course, workshop, or training program, the facilitator will explain whether there is an alternative activity.

4. How to Join the Simulation

You will join the simulation using either:

  1. The QR code shown by the facilitator, or
  2. The shared link provided in the room, email, chat, learning platform, or session page.

5. What to Expect During the Session

The simulation will guide you through a sequence of tasks. Some tasks may be individual. Some tasks may involve a team. Some tasks may allow or require interaction with an AI agent.

The session may include the following stages:

Stage 0: Joining and Waiting

When the simulation first starts, please wait for the facilitator or system to begin the activity. You may see a waiting screen, instructions page, or role assignment screen.

Stage 1: Answering Without AI

You may first complete tasks without AI assistance. In this stage, you should read the scenario carefully, make your own judgment, and explain your reasoning in your own words.

You may be asked to:

  1. Review a business or organizational scenario.
  2. Identify the key issue or decision.
  3. Choose an answer, recommendation, route, or action.
  4. Explain why you made that choice.
  5. Submit your answer before moving to the next task.

Stage 2: Working With an AI Agent

In a later stage, you may be assigned an AI agent or AI-generated output. The AI may provide suggestions, summaries, recommendations, or task guidance.

You may be asked to:

  1. Review the AI agent's output.
  2. Decide whether you agree or disagree with the AI.
  3. Follow, revise, reject, or override the AI's suggestion.
  4. Explain your final decision in your own words.
  5. Submit your final answer.
Please remember: the AI may be helpful, incomplete, or incorrect. You are still responsible for your own judgment during the simulation.

Stage 3: Final Submission or Team Decision

At the end of the simulation, you may be asked to submit a final answer, team decision, reflection, or short explanation of your reasoning.

Stage 4: Debrief and Results

After the session, the facilitator may explain the purpose of the task, common decision patterns, and how the AI assistance affected participant decisions.

  • Your role in the simulation.
  • Your group or team.
  • The stage of the activity.
  • The timing of the session.
  • Whether earlier tasks have been completed.
  • The experimental condition assigned by the study design.

Please complete the tasks shown on your screen in order. If you do not see a task, please wait or notify the facilitator.

7. What You Will Be Asked to Do

During the simulation, please follow these steps:

  1. Read the instructions carefully. Each task will include a prompt, scenario, question, or decision problem.
  2. Complete your assigned task. Make the best decision you can based on the information provided.
  3. Write your reasoning. Explain why you chose your answer. Good reasoning is more important than simply guessing.
  4. Submit your response. Once you submit, you may not be able to revise your answer unless the system allows it.
  5. Wait for the next task. Some stages may require all participants or team members to finish before the next stage begins.
  6. Use AI only when instructed. If a stage says "without AI," please do not use outside AI tools. If a stage provides an AI agent, use only the AI tool provided in the simulation unless the facilitator gives other instructions.
  7. Do not share answers outside your assigned role or team. To keep the simulation fair, please do not tell other participants your answers unless the task specifically asks you to collaborate.

The research team will make reasonable efforts to protect your information.

Your responses may be analyzed for research, educational, product improvement, or training purposes, depending on the approved study protocol. Results may be reported in aggregate form, such as average accuracy, decision patterns, reasoning quality, or group-level comparisons.

However, because open-text responses may contain information you choose to type, please do not include personal, confidential, or sensitive information in your answers.

14. Use of AI in This Simulation

Some stages may involve an AI agent. The AI may produce summaries, suggestions, classifications, recommendations, or reasoning.

Please keep the following in mind:

  • AI output may be inaccurate, incomplete, biased, or misleading.
  • You should evaluate AI output critically.
  • You may agree with, revise, or reject the AI's suggestion when the task allows.
  • Your reasoning should reflect your own judgment, not just repeat the AI's response.
  • The AI agent may not see all participant answers unless the simulation design explicitly says so.

15. Quality Reasoning Guidelines

When writing your answer, please try to:

  1. State your decision clearly.
  2. Explain the evidence or facts that influenced your decision.
  3. Mention any tradeoffs or uncertainty.
  4. Explain why you accepted or rejected AI advice, if AI was used.
  5. Avoid copying text without adding your own judgment.
  6. Keep your response focused on the task.
A strong answer does not need to be long. It should clearly show how you reached your decision.

Participant Guide Sections

What to Expect as a Participant

This simulation will ask you to make decisions in a realistic but fictional scenario. You may complete some tasks independently and some tasks with support from an AI agent. The goal is to understand your reasoning process, not simply whether you get the "right" answer.

Approximate reading time: 5 minutes.

Stage 1: Answering Without AI

In Stage 1, you will answer based on your own judgment. Read carefully, identify the key decision, and explain your reasoning. Do not use outside AI tools unless the facilitator says they are allowed.

Approximate reading time: 7 minutes.

Stage 2: Working With Your AI Agent Effectively

In Stage 2, you may receive help from an AI agent. Treat the AI as an assistant, not as the final decision-maker. Check whether its reasoning fits the evidence. You may follow, revise, or reject its recommendation.

Approximate reading time: 8 minutes.

Results: Understanding Your Session Outcomes

After both stages, the facilitator or research team may evaluate answer accuracy, reasoning depth, decision changes, routing choices, timing, and how AI affected your final answer. Results are usually analyzed at the group or aggregate level.

Approximate reading time: 6 minutes.

FAQ: Common Participant Questions

The FAQ explains whether the task is graded, what to do if you are unsure, whether the AI sees other participants' answers, and what happens to your data.

Approximate reading time: 4 minutes.

Tips: How to Write Quality Decision Reasoning

A good response should clearly state your decision, explain your evidence, mention uncertainty, and show your own judgment. If AI is used, explain whether you accepted or rejected the AI's recommendation and why.
