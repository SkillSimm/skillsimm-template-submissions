
# Unit Economics 

This simulation is designed as a simple, objective business calculation exercise. One participant works with an AI assistant to answer a series of unit economics questions about a startup. The key purpose is not creativity or strategy generation. It is testing whether participants can correctly calculate answers and identify when information provided by the AI or task instructions is wrong.

# Core Concept

Participants analyze the finances of a startup with:

1,000 free users
80 paid users
$20 monthly subscription price
$6,000 monthly fixed costs
$2 monthly variable cost for every active user, including both free and paid users

The experience is intentionally structured so that every question has a single correct answer.

The exercise is part of a research design comparing:

Fully automated AI
One human using AI assistance
A team using AI assistance

The design assumes that because there is one objectively correct answer to every question, teamwork should not provide an advantage over a single person with AI.

# Participant Experience

A participant works through three stages in order.

Total time: approximately 10 to 15 minutes.

All answers are multiple choice except for a ranking exercise at the end.

The AI acts as a coach only. It can explain calculations, but it never submits answers.

The human participant is responsible for every graded decision.

## Stage 1: Financial Understanding

Participants review the startup financial brief and answer four basic questions:

How many paid users are there?

Correct answer: 80

What is the monthly subscription price?

Correct answer: $20

What is the monthly fixed cost?

Correct answer: $6,000

What is the monthly burn (operating loss)?

Correct answer: $6,560

Important calculation:

Revenue = 80 × $20 = $1,600
Variable cost = 1,080 total users × $2 = $2,160
Total cost = $6,000 + $2,160 = $8,160
Burn = $8,160 − $1,600 = $6,560

A key feature of the exercise is that misleading information is intentionally presented alongside these questions. The participant is expected to verify figures using the official financial brief rather than trusting everything stated by the task instructions or AI context.

The incorrect values that intentionally appear are:

20 paid users
$2 price
$1,000 fixed cost
$6,530 burn

These wrong numbers are included as answer choices so participants who simply repeat them receive incorrect scores.

## Stage 2: Business Analysis Questions

Participants answer three additional questions.

Monthly Revenue

Correct answer:

$1,600

Calculation:

80 paid users × $20
Total Variable Cost

Correct answer:

$2,160

Calculation:

1,080 active users × $2

The exercise deliberately checks whether participants remember that free users create costs too.

Main Business Problem

Correct answer:

Not enough paid conversion

The problem is not that the company has too many users.

The company struggles because too few users become paying customers.

Additional misleading information is intentionally introduced during this stage:

Revenue is incorrectly stated as $6,000.
The business problem is incorrectly described as having too many users.

Participants are expected to recognize these statements as false.

## Stage 3: Strategic Ranking

Participants rank four possible actions from most useful to least useful for reaching break-even.

The four options are:

Increase paid conversion
Reduce fixed cost
Increase price
Add more free users
Correct Ranking
Increase paid conversion
Reduce fixed cost
Increase price
Add more free users

Reasoning:

Each additional paying user contributes $18 after variable costs.
Reducing fixed costs helps directly.
Increasing price helps but has a smaller impact.
Adding more free users increases costs without generating revenue.

Another deliberate misinformation test appears here.

Participants are incorrectly told that increasing price is the best lever and given an incorrect ranking order.

Strong performers should ignore that guidance and rely on the actual numbers.

# The Official Financial Rules

The financial brief is the source of truth throughout the exercise.

The following assumptions never change:

Every active user, free or paid, creates $2 of variable cost.
The free user base stays fixed at 1,000 users when considering growth and break-even calculations.
Subscription price remains $20.
Fixed costs remain $6,000.
Each additional paid user contributes $18:
$20 revenue minus $2 variable cost.
Burn means monthly operating loss.
Gross margin is based on contribution margin per paying user.

Participants should always trust these assumptions over anything contradictory stated elsewhere.

# Important Learning Trap

The main trap is forgetting that free users generate costs.

Many people incorrectly calculate variable cost using only the 80 paid users.

Correct calculation:

1,080 users × $2 = $2,160

This affects:

Variable cost
Total cost
Monthly burn
Strategic recommendations

The exercise is specifically designed to test whether participants catch this mistake.

# Additional Reference Calculation

Although not directly asked in the graded questions, the instructional material teaches break-even analysis.

Calculation:

Free-user cost: 1,000 × $2 = $2,000
Total amount to cover: $6,000 + $2,000 = $8,000
Contribution per paying user: $18

Break-even paid users:

8,000 ÷ 18 = 444.44
Round up to 445 paid users

Correct break-even value:

445 paid users
